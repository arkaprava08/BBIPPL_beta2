Prototyping

Rapid Prototyping
As the name implies utilizes various methods to quickly build 3 dimensional, physical models for viewing new products, component aesthetics, and sections, or to confirm part fit. By utilizing rapid prototyping in a product development project, it is possible to shorten project timing and reduce the risk of prototype or production retooling because of assembly fit problems.
Engineering Prototyping
Involves building a physical representation of a part or product for the purposes of evaluating structural integrity, assembly methodology and fit, product performance, and in some cases aesthetics.

Analysis and Optimization

Brick and Byte Structural Analysis Group will offer a complete structural analysis service using conventional and computer aided techniques to assist in the development of new and improved transport and industrial products.

Structural and predictive analysis provide a cost effective method to achieve:
• Design optimization
• Cost/weight reductions
• Material change investigating
• Manufacturing process simulation
• Test simulation

Predictive analysis techniques have advanced to a stage where they can be usefully applied to assess structural concerns from the clay modeling phase to support concern resolution at program development phase. However, the whole analysis process needs to be carefully planned and managed to coordinate input to design at an early stage. Brick and Byte Structures staff will be professional engineers experienced in the application of advanced and classical engineering analysis techniques with practical knowledge of the industries we serve.

The Brick and Byte Structural analysis team will build substantial experience in the following fields of analyses: Stiffness and strength evaluation of metal and composite structures using static linear and nonlinear stress analysis for lighter, stronger, more efficient design.

Dynamic nonlinear analysis for the prediction and evaluation of impacts or blasts related to all types of transport or safety engineering.
